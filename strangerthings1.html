Stranger Things season 1

The first season of the American science fiction horror drama television series Stranger Things premiered worldwide on the streaming service Netflix on July 15, 2016.[1] Producted by Monkey Massacre Productions and 21 Laps Entertainment, the series was created by the Duffer Brothers, who also serve as executive producers along with Shawn Levy and Dan Cohen. Karl Gajdusek also serves as an executive producer and co-showrunner for the season. The season stars Winona Ryder, David Harbour, Millie Bobby Brown, Caleb McLaughlin, Natalia Dyer, Charlie Heaton, Cara Buono, and Matthew Modine, with Joe Keery, Noah Schnapp and Shannon Purser appearing in recurring roles. Set in 1983, the first season plot revolves around the citizens of Hawkins searching for the missing Will Byers (Schnapp), inadvertently uncovering the supernatural in the town, which is related to secret experiments conducted at the local Hawkins laboratory. With that, the citizens ally themselves with one of the laboratory's human test subjects — a girl with psychokinetic and telepathic abilities named Eleven (Brown) - who escaped from the laboratory, helping them find Will in a parallel dimension known as the Upside Down. After they gained experience writing some episodes of Wayward Pines, the Duffer Brothers pitched the series to numerous cable networks until Levy, founder of 21 Laps Entertainment, bought the rights and granted full authorship to the brothers. The concept for the season is primarily inspired by classic science fiction and horror media, particularly those of the 1980s, the period that inspired the series' characterization. Filming began on September 25, 2015, and concluded in early 2016, taking place largely in Atlanta, Georgia. The season was released for Home Video in two packages in 2017; the first, containing DVD and Blu-ray, was released in October, and the second, containing Blu-ray on 4K, in November.
